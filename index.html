About Me

My name is Zach Bar, and writing this right now reminds me of the infamous interview question: Tell me a little about yourself. I hate it. That's why I won't do that, and instead, I'll walk you through my life, starting with my early adolescence.

Born in Israel, where a large proportion of my relatives live, I grew up speaking Hebrew and puzzles. Since my infantry, I was a problem-solver. Building puzzles far above my age range was a minor task for me and I created patterns out of the toy cars I was given to play with. My parents talk about it to this day.

Now, let's cut to present day Zach Bar. To some I've lived up to my expectation. Kid can solve patterns -> Computer Science major is an easy logical structure for most to understand. Others might say I've failed to meet my expectations. Community college doesn't have the pretty name 4-year universities do.

Despite the setbacks, I never thought of myself as incapable of understanding the fundamentals of computer programming. After all, I never thought something could be more cool than to build your own app! So I worked towards it. I took what I had learned from school, and what I learned by utilizing online resources, and combined it to create my own understanding.

This really got me into web-development, particularly front-end design. I believe I have come to learn a lot about framework languages like HTML and CSS, and am thrilled also to find myself creating a fun environment for website visitors with JavaScript. It really captured me, and led me to like to program like never before.

On my way to master web design, I stumbled on a little game design as well. Now having been a sensei at Code Ninjas for seven months, I can not only teach game design with JavaScript and Unity, but I can also babysit, for all you parents out there. With these learning experiences paired with what I've learned in college, I am hopeful that I can work so far in game development and web design, and pass the questions about data manipulation in the interview that is completely unnecessary for either of them.

In the future, I do plan to make use of what I've learned from school as well. I'm sure it could help me with a variety of things, especially that of which is consistent with app development, something that I strive to learn more about. I am currently learning Swift, a language used to build apps created by Apple. I'm hoping to build my first app soon, and hopefully finally start making actual money from this two year time expenditure!

As can be seen from all the above examples, as well as those that I chose to leave out (no need for information overload), I'm sure everyone reading this can testify to my work ethic and love to learn, even if it's all from word of mouth. Anyway, that's all I can really say about myself without writing an entire book. Thanks to everyone reading this, and since you got this far, I'll let you in on a little secret: I'm currently working on developing this section into an interactive day in my life game, so stay tuned for that!

Signing off,
Zach
